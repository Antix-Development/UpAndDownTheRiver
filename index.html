Up & Down The River.

About


Up and Down the River is a social card game best enjoyed after dinner with good friends, easy listening music, and a few beverages of your choice.

The game is played in rounds, with each round consisting of a number of tricks. The game style is pretty much identical to the well known card game Euchre.

When playing "up the river" the number of cards dealt to each player increases from one card in the first round, to the number of cards chosen as the length of the river in the last round.

Then the game is played back "down the river" starting with the maximum number of cards, to one card in the last round.

You should be able to see where the name "Up and Down the River" comes from now.

How to Play


The deck is shuffled and cards are dealt cards face up to all players in order until a Jack is dealt, and the player who received the jack becomes the dealer for the first round.

In the application choose the number of players and length of the river. Then enter the names of the players starting with the dealer and proceeding clockwise after that.

The dealer gathers and shuffles the deck, and then deals the required number of cards for the round to each player.

The dealer then turns the next card in the deck face up, and this card is the trump suit.

When played, a trump card "trumps" (or beats) any other card of any other suit, no matter what that cards face value is. For example, if hearts is trump then the Jack of Hearts (known as the left bower) is the highest card in the game, then followed by its opposite red card, the Jack of Diamonds (known as the right bower), then the Ace of Hearts, King of Hearts, and so on.

With the trump suit established, each player examines their cards determines how many tricks they will claim in the current round, and selects this number in the application.

The last player to claim tricks is the dealer where some caveats apply.

- If the total number of tricks claimed is equal to the maximum number of tricks in the round, then the dealer cannot claim zero tricks, they must claim at least one trick.

- The dealer cannot claim a number of tricks if the total number of tricks claimed plus their desired claim equals the maximum number of tricks in the round, so if there is a maximum of 10 tricks and the total number of claimed tricks is 7, then the dealer cannot claim 3 tricks.

Once all players have informed the keeper how many tricks they claim, the round begins with the lead player laying down a card from their hand.

All other players (in the established order) then play a card and where possible must follow suit, so if the lead player plays a Heart, then all other players MUST also play a Heart if they have one in their hand.

If any player cannot follow suit then they are free to play any card in their hand.

Once all players have laid down a card, the winner is determined and they claim the trick.

The winner of the trick now becomes the lead player and the round continues as described above, until all tricks have been claimed.

Once a round is complete, players are awarded points according to how many tricks they claimed versus how many tricks they actually took.

if a player takes the number of tricks that they claimed then they are awarded 10 points and an additional 1 point for each trick they took.

if a player claims 0 tricks and they take 0 zero tricks then they are awarded 5 points

if a player does not take the number of tricks they claimed then they are not awarded any points.

Once all rounds have been played the winner is the player with the most accumulated points.
